The Story of Kaka

"Nostalgia brewed fresh daily. Conversations served warm."

KakaBabu was envisioned in 1969 by our beloved 'Kaka' (Uncle) — a storyteller, and tea enthusiast who believed that the best ideas are born over clinking tea cups and hot cutlets. He designed KakaBabu not merely as a culinary stop, but as a sanctuary for *adda* — the legendary Bengali art of informal, soulful conversation.

Today, we have reimagined Kaka's original tea cabin for the modern era. We've preserved the essence of Kalyani's heritage — the warmth, the spices, and the legendary recipes — and infused them into a sleek, contemporary bistro space.

Here, clean lines meet warm turmeric glow, and traditional clay cups sit beautifully alongside modern pour-overs. Pull up a chair, order a classic cutlet, and share a story. You belong to the adda now.
